# Seedance 2.0 — Scenario Patterns (English)

> On-demand module. Hard limits and the loop live in SKILL.md.

Each pattern names a common shape and shows an original worked prompt. Adapt the wording; keep the structure.

### 1. Holding a character across shots
Anchor the look to one image and let the action flow.
```
The violinist in @Image1 waits in the wings, eyes closed, rolling her
shoulders loose. Tight on her hands as she tightens a peg and tests a
string. She exhales, opens her eyes with quiet resolve, and steps toward
the stage light. Warm backstage glow, faint murmur of an audience.
```

### 2. Borrowing a camera path
Lift the exact movement from a reference clip.
```
Lead is @Image1's skateboarder, in @Image2's concrete underpass. Borrow
@Video1's camera path move-for-move — a low follow glide beside the
deck, then a whip pan as he kicks the board up. Keep his expression
focused, almost bored. Echo of wheels on concrete.
```

### 3. Replicating a creative template / effect
Swap the subject into a reference's effect language.
```
Replace @Video1's subject with @Image1; start on @Image1. A free-diver
pushes off a cliff edge. Match @Video1's transition exactly — the camera
follows her down and breaks the water surface into @Image2's coral reef.
She glides through a kelp gate into @Image3's sunken hull, dust of light
falling around her. Muffled underwater hush throughout.
```

### 4. Extending a clip forward
Continue an existing video; set generation length to the new piece only.
```
Extend @Video1 by 15 seconds.
1–5s    A record needle settles; the vinyl turns, dust drifts in a slant
        of afternoon light.
6–10s   The camera eases toward the spinning label until the frame dims.
11–15s  Type fades up: "NIGHT OWL RECORDS", then "open till late".
```
For a backward extension, prepend instead:
```
Extend backward 10s: dawn light climbs a mountain ridge, the camera tilts
slowly down to a single tent with smoke curling from a small fire.
```

### 5. Editing an existing clip
Keep most of the footage; change a defined thing.

Reverse the beat:
```
Rework @Video1's tone — the interviewer's warm smile cools to something
flat. Without a word he slides a single sheet across the desk and leans
back. The candidate's hope drains. No raised voices, just the scrape of
paper and a long silence.
```
Swap a subject:
```
Replace the runner in @Video1 with @Image1's cyclist; keep the route and
cuts identical, no new shots.
```
Add an element:
```
Behind the character in @Video1, a drift of paper lanterns lifts off and
rises into the dusk sky.
```

### 6. Cutting to a beat
Sync stills to a track.
```
@Image1 @Image2 @Image3 @Image4 @Image5 @Image6 — sneaker hero shots.
Cut them to the keyframes and overall pulse of @Video1. Add a little
motion to each frame, push contrast and a flash of rim light on impact.
Adjust framing per shot as the rhythm needs.
```

### 7. Dialogue and voicing
Write the lines and the delivery.
```
"Office Plants, After Hours" — a dry two-hander.
Cactus (unbothered, slow): "You wilted again. I told you, less drama,
less water."
Fern (offended, rustling): "Oh, rich, coming from the one who hasn't
grown a millimeter since spring."
Deadpan comedic timing, soft office ambience after the lights cut.
```

### 8. The unbroken take
One shot, no cuts.
```
@Image1 @Image2 @Image3 @Image4 — a single take following a waiter with a
loaded tray out of a clattering kitchen, down a service corridor, through
swinging doors, onto a rooftop terrace at golden hour. No cuts.
```
With location changes inside the oner:
```
Start on @Image1. The camera tracks a courier weaving a moped through a
narrow old-town lane, full shot, stalls and pedestrians crossing frame.
At the corner — @Image2's archway — he brakes, hands a parcel to a figure
referencing @Image3, then the camera pans up to follow him vanishing into
@Image4's market crowd. One continuous take, no cuts.
```

### 9. Product showcase
Make the object the hero.

Exploded assembly:
```
Locked-off camera, soft seamless backdrop. The earbud case in @Image1
floats and opens; both buds rise out and rotate. The case separates into
clean layered components — shell, battery, board — holding their spacing
in mid-air, then everything glides back together into one sealed case.
Smooth, no gimmicks, premium product light, faint magnetic click on close.
```
Spin to reveal:
```
The sneaker in @Image1 spins twice at speed, snaps to a stop, then peels
into upper, midsole, and outsole hovering apart for a beat before
slamming back whole. 3D render look, crisp impact accents.
```

### 10. Explainer / educational
Visualize an idea cleanly.
```
15-second water-cycle explainer.
0–5s    A cross-section of a valley; the sun warms a river and wisps of
        vapor lift and gather into a cloud.
5–10s   The cloud thickens and cools; rain falls over the ridge, threads
        of water running back toward the river.
10–15s  Pull wide to show the full loop — sea, sky, rain, river — labeled
        with simple arrows. Clean, calm, instructional palette.
```

### 11. Comic / micro-drama adaptation
Bring a page or board to life.
```
Animate @Image1 panel by panel, left to right, top to bottom. Keep each
character's spoken line identical to the page. Punctuate panel changes and
key beats with sharp foley; light, witty tone. Performance style follows
@Video1.
```
Generate a board-driven opener:
```
Using @Image1's storyboard as the guide — its panels, framing, camera and
captions — make a 15s opening titled "City at Dawn", gentle and warm.
```

### 12. Fusing clips together
Bridge several videos into one flow.
```
A drop of ink blooms in water in @Video1; as it spreads it dissolves into
@Video2, a flower opening in time-lapse; the petals scatter and reform as
@Video3's slow spiral of a galaxy, then fade. Dreamlike throughout, with a
low ambient drone and soft chimes.
```

---

## Mixing patterns (not a fixed menu)
- First/last frame + a reference's motion: `start on @Image1, copy @Video1's fight choreography`
- Extend then keep going: `extend @Video1 by 5s` (also select 5s)
- Bridge clips: `add a scene between @Video1 and @Video2 showing …`
- No audio file but want a sound feel: reference the sound inside a video directly
- Chain a continuous action: `she goes straight from the vault into a roll, unbroken` + `@Image1 @Image2 @Image3 …`
