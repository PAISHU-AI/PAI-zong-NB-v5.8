# Copywriting Content

## Purpose

Create or refine user-facing text with a clear audience, tone, and action.

Use this for:
- Product copy, landing-page sections, feature descriptions.
- Release notes, changelogs, announcements, social posts.
- Naming, slogans, taglines, short-form text.
- Rewriting for tone, clarity, persuasion, or structure.

## Workflow

1. Identify audience, channel, goal, tone, and required length.
2. Preserve factual claims from provided material; do not invent metrics, customers, awards, or guarantees.
3. Produce the requested format directly.
4. Offer alternatives only when the task benefits from options, naming, or tone exploration.
5. For technical or product claims, mark assumptions or request source material when claims matter.

## Required Checks

- Match the user's language unless they ask otherwise.
- Keep claims verifiable and avoid fake authority.
- Avoid generic AI filler, empty praise, and over-stylized marketing language.
- If this content will become a reusable style rule, hand off to `zhuluyou` or `skill-distillation-pro`.

## Avoid

- Do not over-explain writing choices unless asked.
- Do not add emojis, memes, or slang unless requested.
- Do not create legal, medical, financial, or compliance claims without source-backed review.

## Output

Return the final copy first. Add a short note only when assumptions, variants, or source gaps matter.
